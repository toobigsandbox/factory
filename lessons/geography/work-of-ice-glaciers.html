Geography // Work of Water

Work of Ice: Glaciers

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To observe how a massive, moving body of ice (a glacier) carves and transforms the landscape into a distinct U-shaped valley.

Materials

  • Clay: Soft and pliable, formed into a small "V-shaped" valley.
  • Ice Block: A large, rectangular block (frozen in a small plastic container).
  • Heavy Weight: A brick or a heavy stone to place on top of the ice.
  • Photos: Glacial Valleys (e.g., Yosemite) vs. River Valleys.

Presentation

Inventory Note: AMI Geography Charts depicting the "River of Ice" and Moraines are copyright protected. Use the physical charts as the control of error for the child's follow-up work.
Gather the children and show the clay valley. Trace the "V" shape with your finger.
"We have seen how a river carves a sharp path through the mountains. But sometimes, when it is very cold, the water doesn't move as a liquid. It moves as a massive solid: a glacier."
Place the ice block at the top of the clay valley. Place the heavy weight on top of the ice.
"A glacier is very heavy. Because of its weight and the pull of gravity, it begins to slide down the mountain. It moves so slowly we can hardly see it, but it is very strong."
Fig 9.1: Modeling the pressure and mass of a glacier
Push the ice block slowly through the clay valley, allowing it to scrape the sides and bottom.
"Look at what the ice is doing to our sharp 'V' valley. It is scraping away the earth. It is carrying everything in its path—rocks, dirt, even pieces of the mountain."
Remove the ice and weight. Observe the new "U" shape of the valley.
"The glacier has changed the shape of the mountain forever. It is no longer a sharp 'V'; it is a wide, deep 'U'. This is how we know where a 'River of Ice' once lived."
Fig 9.2: Comparative valley morphology
"Guide Tip: Highlight the 'Moraine'—the pile of dirt left at the end of the ice's journey. This helps the children understand that the glacier doesn't just carve; it carries and deposits just like a river, only with much more force."
