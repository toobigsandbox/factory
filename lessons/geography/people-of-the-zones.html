Geography // Human Adaptations

People of the Different Zones

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To explore how humans adapt their housing, clothing, and lifestyle to satisfy their fundamental needs within different climate zones.

Materials

  • Photos/Models: Showing traditional and indigenous housing and clothing.
  • World Map: To locate the Torrid, Temperate, and Frigid zones.
  • Chart 27 (Population Overlay): To reference settlement areas.

Presentation

Review the three major zones. Discuss the Torrid zone.
"In the hot and wet areas, people often built their houses on stilts to keep away from the moisture and the mud. They wore light clothing that allowed the air to move."
Fig 11.1: Human adaptation of shelter across zones
"In the Frigid zone, people had to use their brains to find solutions in a harsh climate. They built houses from ice or sod and wore thick furs or synthetic layers to keep the heat inside."
"Guide Tip: This is the 'human' side of the Work of Water. It shows that although the environment is powerful, humans are creative problem solvers who can thrive anywhere by understanding and cooperating with nature."
