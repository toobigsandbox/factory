Geography // Work of Water

Work of Ice: The Power of Expansion

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To demonstrate the immense force created when water expands as it freezes, and to relate this force to the weathering of mountains (frost wedging).

Materials

  • Glass Bottle: Small, with a screw-top or cork.
  • Water: To fill the bottle to the absolute brim.
  • Towel or Plastic Bag: To contain the pieces for safety.
  • Freezer: Access needed for overnight observation.

Presentation

Gather the children. Show the glass bottle.
"We have seen water carve the earth as a liquid. But water has a secret power when it gets very cold. It needs more space."
Fill the bottle with water until it is overflowing slightly (the meniscus).
"I am filling this bottle to the very top. There is no room left for even one more drop of water."
Seal the bottle tightly. Wrap it in a towel or place it in a clear bag.
"We will put this in the freezer tonight. If the water decides it needs more room, let's see what it does to this strong glass bottle."
Fig 8.1: The force of freezing water shattering glass
Remove the wrapped bottle the next morning and open it carefully with the children.
"Look! The water expanded so much that it broke the glass. Imagine if this water was inside a tiny crack in a massive mountain. When it freezes, it can split even the hardest rock in two."
"Guide Tip: This experiment is much more visceral than the sponge. The sound of the glass cracking is the 'Voice of Ice.' Ensure you follow all safety protocols (the bag is essential!) so the children can see the results without danger."
