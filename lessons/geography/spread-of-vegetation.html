Geography // Work of Water

The Spread of Vegetation & Population

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To synthesize knowledge of climate, water, and vegetation to understand the logic behind human settlement patterns on Earth.

Materials

  • AMI Chart 27: The Vegetation Chart (Copyright Protected).
  • Population Density Overlay: Transparent sheet with red dots.
  • Plant Specimens/Photos: Showing specific leaf adaptations.

Presentation

Gather children and review the climate zones. Point to the Equatorial (Torrid) band.
"In the wet rainforests, plants grow massive leaves to breathe out extra water. In the hot deserts, they grow needles or thick skin to keep water in."
Fig 10.1: Biological adaptations to water levels
Place the transparent Red Dot Overlay on top of the Vegetation Chart.
"Look at these red dots. They show us where large groups of people live. Do you notice how they follow the green bands and the rivers?"
Fig 10.2: Correlation between resources and settlement
"Most people settle where it is 'hospitable'—not too hot, not too cold, and always where there is enough water to support life."
"Guide Tip: This is the moment of synthesis. The children realize that human history is not accidental; it is built on the laws of geography and biology."
Elim Lee // Semester 4 Sprint // AMI Elementary
