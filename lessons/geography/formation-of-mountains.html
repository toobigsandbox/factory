Geography // Geology

Formation of Mountains

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To explore the different ways mountains and valleys are formed by the movements of the Earth's crust.

Materials

Stack of felt sheets, solid geometry material (that divides), two rectangular prisms, yellow volume material pieces, charts/pictures of mountain types.

Presentation

Show pictures of old volcanic mountains.
"Some mountains are dead volcanoes. The old lava remains as a raised piece of land, usually in a cone shape that rises suddenly from flat land."
Fig 1.1: Volcanic cones vs. Folded strata
Place felt sheets flat in layers. Gently push from both ends.
"Most mountains are formed by pressure. As the land gets pushed together, it folds like these felt layers. These are called fold mountains."
Use two prisms with matching patterns. Slide one slightly up or slanted.
"When the patterns don't line up anymore because of pressure, we call these cracks 'faults'. They can be vertical or slanted (oblique)."
Fig 2.1: Displaced rock layers at a fault line
Arrange three blocks. Lower the middle section for a Rift Valley; raise the middle for a Block Mountain (Horst).
"A rift valley forms when land sinks between faults. A block mountain, or horst, forms when the land between cracks rises up."
Fig 3.1: Comparative crustal movement
"If a child asks how to distinguish them, remind them to look for the relationship: if the land between the faults is lower, it is a valley; if it is higher, it is a horst."
