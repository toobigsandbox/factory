Geography // Work of Water

Ocean Waves: The Great Sculptor

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To observe the rhythmic power of waves in creating landforms and to identify these coastal patterns (Gulfs, Bays, Peninsulas) on the global map.

Materials

  • Wave Tray: Long tray with sand/clay cliff and water.
  • Paddle: To create rhythmic motion.
  • World Map/Atlas: (See Copyright Note).
  • Photos: Sea Caves, Arches, Stacks, Gulfs, and Peninsulas.

Part I: The Action of the Sea

Create gentle waves in the tray. Observe the impact on the sandy cliff.
"The ocean is always moving. Every wave strikes the land, carving out caves, then arches, and finally leaving lonely stacks of rock standing in the water."
Fig 6.1: The progression of coastal erosion

Part II: The Kiss of the Sea (Nomenclature)

Point to a map of a jagged coastline (e.g., Greece or the Gulf of Mexico).
"Where the sea has pushed deep into the land, we call it a Gulf. Where the land reaches out, surrounded by water, we call it a Peninsula."
Fig 6.2: Coastal nomenclature — Top-down abstraction
"Guide Tip: This lesson provides the vocabulary for the child's independent map work. The goal is to see the 'Great Sculptor' in every curve of the shore."
