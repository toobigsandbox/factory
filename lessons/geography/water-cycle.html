Geography // Work of Water

The Water Cycle

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To understand the cosmic recycling of water through evaporation, condensation, and precipitation—the "Giant Slide" that keeps the Earth's work moving.

Materials

  • Electric Tea Kettle or hot plate with a pot of water.
  • Clear Glass Lid or a clear tray.
  • Ice Cubes in a bowl.
  • Chart 11: The Water Cycle (See Copyright Note).

Presentation

Inventory Note: Montessori Geography Chart 11 (The Water Cycle) is a copyrighted visual and is not included in this digital repository. It should be placed as the control of error for the child's independent drawings.
Gather the children and turn on the kettle. Wait for the steam to rise.
"We have seen the river carve the mountains. But how does the water get back to the top of the mountain to start its work again? The Sun acts as a great engine, lifting the water up into the sky."
Fig 5.1: The Sun lifting water from the surface
Hold the clear lid above the escaping steam.
"This steam is water that has turned into a gas. It is so light it can float! But as it goes higher into the atmosphere, it gets colder."
Place ice cubes on top of the clear lid. Observe the underside of the lid.
"Look at the bottom of our 'cloud'. The gas is turning back into liquid. The little droplets are gathering together. This is 'Condensation'."
Fig 5.2: Ice inducing condensation and "rainfall"
Watch as the heavy droplets fall back into the kettle.
"When the droplets get too heavy for the air to hold, they fall. They hit the mountaintops and slide back down to the sea. It is like a giant slide that never ends!"
"Guide Tip: This is the perfect moment to discuss how water can be 'reused' for millions of years. The water the child drinks today might be the same water that once traveled through a dinosaur!"
