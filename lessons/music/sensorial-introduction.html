Music // The Tone Bars

Sensorial Introduction to the Tone Bars

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To introduce the tone bars as a sensorial tool for auditory refinement and to discover the mathematical pattern of the major scale, which can begin on any pitch.

Materials

  • 25 Tone Bars: Covering two octaves, in black and white holders.
  • Keyboard Boards: Painted to resemble the piano keyboard.
  • Hard Rubber Mallet and damper.
  • Major Scale Pattern Strip: Numbered 1-8, establishing the diatonic pattern.

Part I: Matching and Grading

Invite the children to the rug. Randomize two sets of white tone bars.
"You have worked with the bells to find twin sounds. The tone bars work the same way, but they look like a keyboard. Let's find the matching pairs."
Strike a control bar, then search for its match in the second set.
"These two sounds are exactly the same. They are twins."
Invite the child to grade one set from the lowest pitch to the highest pitch.
"Now we have a staircase of sound. It begins deep and low and climbs to the high notes."

Part II: The Major Scale Pattern

Place the bars in keyboard order on the boards. Introduce the Major Scale Pattern Strip ($1-8$).
"Every major scale follows a secret code of steps. We can use this numbered strip to find the notes for any scale, starting anywhere we like."
Place the strip starting on 'C'. Play the bars indicated by the numbers.
"When we start on C, the numbers land on all the white bars. This is the C Major Scale."
Fig 1.2: Using the pattern strip to identify scale tones
Move the strip to start on 'G'. Show how one number now lands on a black bar.
"If we want the scale to sound the same but start on G, the pattern tells us we must use this black bar. The pattern is the law that keeps the music beautiful."
"Note to the Guide: This lesson bridges sensorial refinement with musical theory. Ensure the children understand that the distance between bars (whole steps and half steps) is what the numbered strip is actually measuring. Always handle bars by the wooden base to protect the pitch."
