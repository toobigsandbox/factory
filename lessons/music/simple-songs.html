Music // Tone Bars: Major Scales

Simple Songs

Prepared by: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

  • To provide opportunities for exploration and practice using the major scale in playing simple, recognizable songs.
  • To strengthen the child’s ear-hand connection in music-making.
  • To foster joy, confidence, and independence through successful music experiences.
  • To connect sensorial exploration (tone bars, scale) with musical literacy (numbers, notation).

Material Analysis

  • Tone Bars: A full diatonic set of tuned metal bars representing the $C$ major scale ($C_4$ to $C_5$), mounted on separate wooden blocks.
  • Rubber Mallet: A specialized striker with a soft, round rubber head to produce a warm, lingering resonance.
  • Major Scale Pattern Strip: A movable white guide strip labeled with the scale degree numbers $1$ to $8$ aligned underneath the bars.
  • Simple Songs Envelope: A packet containing individual cards with familiar melodies represented as sequences of numbers (e.g., $3 - 2 - 1$).

Presentation

The Guide invites the child to the Music shelves, introduces the Tone Bars, and shows the "Simple Songs" envelope.
"Now that we know how to use this strip with our tone bars—and how to make our tone bar sound beautiful—we can learn how to play a few simple songs."
The Guide removes the envelope labeled 'Simple Songs' from the shelf and holds it before the child.
"On our shelf, we have this envelope, and it has simple songs in it. They’re written using numbers, so we’re going to choose one. I will choose this song."
The Guide pulls out a song card (e.g., 'Hot Cross Buns'), places it flat on the table, and pulls down the corresponding tone bars.
"What I’m going to do is place this where I can see it and read it. So, there it is, right there. I’ll just leave my strip right here and pull down my tone bars."
The Guide strikes the starting tonic bar (1) gently with the mallet.
"Let me just make sure I’ve done it correctly... [Plays tone bar 1]... Sounds like it!"
Fig 4.1: Sensory connection of numbers to scale degrees
The Guide carefully reads the numbered song card, modeling absolute focal alignment, and plays the melody.
"Shall I read and play? [Plays melody for 'Hot Cross Buns']... That’s Hot Cross Buns, isn’t it?"
The Guide shifts the scale pattern strip to begin on a different starting key, showing transposition sensory-style.
"Let’s move our strip. I’ll put the tone bars back. Now, move the strip anywhere you want to, and we’ll see if it still sounds like Hot Cross Buns."
The Guide plays the same numerical sequence starting on the new base key.
"Does that sound like Hot Cross Buns? Yeah—it does! You can move the strip anywhere you want, and play a song. Then move the strip again. Have fun!"
