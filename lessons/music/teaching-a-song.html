Music // Instructional Pedagogy

Steps for Teaching a Song

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To provide a scaffolded framework for introducing music that supports memory, rhythm, and enunciation through intentional steps of listening and imitation.

Presentation

Step 1: Name the Song & Step 2: Play the Starting Pitch.
"This song is called 'Black Socks'. Let's listen to the starting pitch on the tone bars. Even if I don't match the pitch perfectly, playing it helps some of you find it, and then I can match your voices."
Step 3: Introduce the Melody & Step 4: Discuss Vocabulary.
"First, just listen to the melody. Now, let's look at the words. Does everyone know what 'murmurs' means? It means to speak very softly—this tells us how we should sing that part of the song."
Step 5: Say the Song in Rhythm & Step 6: Call-and-Response.
"I am going to recite the words in rhythm without singing. Now, I’ll say a line and you repeat it back to me. This helps us practice our listening and pacing."
Fig 2.1: The Call-and-Response Loop (Step 6)
Step 7: Sing With the Children & Step 8: Say the Whole Song Together.
"Now we sing it together, and I will guide you with my voice. Next, let's say the whole song in rhythm again, everyone at once."
Step 9: Say the Song Silently & Step 10: Adult Sings, Children Mouth Words.
"This time, mouth the words silently with me. Watch my mouth carefully—this helps us with enunciation. Now, keep mouthing the words while you listen to me sing the melody alone."
Step 11: Sing All Together & Step 12: Repeat as Needed.
"Let’s sing the whole song together now! We can repeat it until it feels like a musical friend we know well."
Step 13: Name the Song Again.
"That was 'Black Socks'. Let's write that name on our 'Songs We Know' list so we can invite it back to our circle another time."
"Note: This is a flexible framework. You don’t need every step every time—adjust for context. Aim to teach 1-2 songs per week. Repetitive lyrics are vital; they allow students who 'fall off' to jump back in easily. Keep the 'Songs We Know' list visible to ensure a rotating repertoire."
Elim Lee // Singing Module // AMI Elementary
