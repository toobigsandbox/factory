Music // Instructional Pedagogy

Steps for Teaching a Song

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To provide a structural framework for introducing new music, ensuring that memory, enunciation, and community engagement are supported through a flexible instructional protocol.

Presentation Protocol

Step 1: Name the Song & Play Starting Pitch.
"This song is called 'Black Socks'. Let's hear the starting pitch on the tone bars so we begin together."
Steps 2-4: Introduce Melody, Vocabulary, and Rhythm.
"First, listen to the whole melody. Are there words you don't know? 'Murmurs' means to speak softly—that tells us how to sing it. Now, let's say the words together in rhythm without the music."
Steps 5-7: Call-and-Response and Shared Singing.
"I will say a line, and you repeat it back. Now, let's sing the whole thing together. I will guide you with my voice."
Steps 8-10: Silent Mouth and Guided Listening.
"Now we say the song silently—only mouth the words. This helps us enunciate. Now, listen while I sing and you only mouth the words."
Steps 11-12: Full Performance and Re-Naming.
"Let's sing it all together with our full voices! That was 'Black Socks'. Let's add it to our 'Songs We Know' list."
The Cycle of Song Acquisition
"The goal is one new song per week. Repetitive songs are best for younger children as they provide 'on-ramps' if they get lost. Keep a visible list of known songs to rotate them and prevent boredom."
Elim Lee // Singing Module // AMI Elementary
