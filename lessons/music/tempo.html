Music // Time & Speed

Tempo

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

LessonID: MOV-06

Anchor: TRANSCRIPT_MOV-06

Purpose

To provide an opportunity for the child to understand tempo through whole‑body movement, singing, and instrument playing.

Materials

Tone bars, Cards with tempo markings (Adagio, Allegro, Andante, etc.).

Presentation

Introduce the three main tempos: Andante, Adagio, Allegro.
"Andante means walking. Allegro means happy or fast. Adagio means slow."
Play a simple song on the tone bars at varying tempos.
"How does your walking change when I play this Andante? What about Allegro?"
Introduce Accelerando and Ritardando.
"Sometimes we get faster and faster (Accelerando) or slower and slower (Ritardando)."
"Tempo is the heartbeat of music. In Montessori, we always begin with the body's natural pace—the 'Andante'—before exploring the extremes of speed."
Elim Lee // April 1st Sprint // AMI Elementary
