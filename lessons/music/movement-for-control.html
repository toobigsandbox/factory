Movement for Control

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

LessonID: MOV-02

Anchor: TRANSCRIPT_MOV-02

Purpose

  • To support the child in developing controlled, intentional movement.
  • To establish ground rules and structured exercises for movement refinement.

Materials

4 sets of exercises (as described in presentation).

Presentation

Establish the "Golden Rule" of movement.
"In this room, we move freely, but we never touch anyone or anything unless it is part of the work."

The Four Exercises

Exercise 1: Walk freely; stop immediately when the signal is given.
"This is about inhibitory control—the ability to stop our motion at will."
Exercise 2: Walk at different speeds (Slow, Fast, Normal).
"Can you change your speed without losing your balance?"
Exercise 3: Walk with high knees, on tiptoes, or on heels.
Exercise 4: The Silence Exercise.
"Let us sit and create a collective silence. Notice your breath and the stillness of your limbs."
"Ground rules are essential. If movement becomes chaotic, it is usually because the exercises lack clear structure or the adult has lost the 'leader' signal. Consistency is the key to freedom."
Elim Lee // April 1st Sprint // AMI Elementary
