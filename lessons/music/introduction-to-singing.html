Music // Vocal Expression

Introduction to Singing

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To present singing as an intentional, regular, and integrated part of classroom life; to establish music as a "second language" that builds community, memory, and joy.

The Narrative Approach

Gather the children and open the discussion on Language.
"We all agree on how important it is to read aloud together. Why do we do it? Because we have a sensitivity to the sounds, patterns, and rhythms of language. Music is simply another language—one we are hardwired for, just like we are hardwired for food."
Fig 1.1: Music and Language as parallel tracks of human expression
Discuss the value of the voice as a communal tool.
"When we sing, we bring our voices together. We create community. Research from UW-Madison tells us that singing captivates us even more than speaking because it combines the expression on our faces with the tone of our voices. It doesn't matter if the singing is technically 'perfect'—it's the connection that matters."
Invite the children to consider the diversity of song.
"Music allows us to connect to the human story. We can sing a song from the people we are studying in History, or music from a part of the world we are visiting in Geography. It fills our transition times and redirects our energy with joy."
"If you are not yet confident in your singing voice, try singing along with a recording or having an assistant sing near you to pull you musically while you lead. Remember: Even imperfect voices build perfect moments of connection. Focus on simple melodies and repetition for 6-9, and expand into complex rhythms and longer ranges for 9-12."
Final Encouragement.
"We will sing every day. It will be our rhythm. Let's start with a song we all know."
Elim Lee // Singing Module // AMI Elementary
