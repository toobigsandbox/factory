Music // Vocal Expression

Introduction to Singing

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To introduce the importance of singing as an integral part of classroom life and community, emphasizing that music is a second language hardwired into the human experience.

Presentation

Gather the children and draw a parallel to reading aloud.
"We all agree on the importance of reading aloud. We listen to the patterns and rhythms of language. Music is simply another language, and our classroom is not complete without singing."
Fig 1.1: Music as a parallel language of rhythm and pattern
Discuss why singing matters for community.
"Singing allows us to bring our voices together, create community, and express joy. Even if you don't think you have the 'best' voice, we still sing. Research from UW–Madison shows singing captivates children more than speaking because it combines facial expression and vocal tone."
Connect music to culture and history.
"Music connects us to the human story. We might sing a song from a culture we are studying or an oral tradition from another part of the world. It is a way of saying: 'Here is how these people felt.'"
"Building confidence is part of the Guide's work. If you are hesitant, sing along with a recording or have an assistant help lead. Remember: children aren't looking for a 'performer'; they are looking for a community member sharing a joy. Silly songs and movement help bridge the gap for older children (9-12)."
Elim Lee // Singing Module // AMI Elementary
