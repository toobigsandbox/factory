Music // Expression & Volume

Dynamics

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

LessonID: MOV-05

Anchor: TRANSCRIPT_MOV-05

Purpose

  • To illustrate the various marks used in music for dynamics.
  • To help the child understand dynamics through whole‑body movement, singing, and instrument playing.

Materials

Cards illustrating: forte, piano, crescendo, decrescendo.

Presentation

Introduce the terms Forte (loud) and Piano (soft).
"Forte means strong. Let's walk with heavy, strong steps. Piano means quiet. Let's move like we are on clouds."
Demonstrate Crescendo and Decrescendo.
"Watch my hands. As they move apart, sing the note louder (Crescendo). As they come together, get softer (Decrescendo)."
"Dynamics are not just 'volume.' They are the emotional state of the music. A 'piano' section might be peaceful or it might be tense. Encourage the children to discuss how the volume affects their mood."
Elim Lee // April 1st Sprint // AMI Elementary
