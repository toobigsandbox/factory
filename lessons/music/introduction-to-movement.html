Music // Movement Foundations

Introduction to Movement

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

LessonID: MOV-01

Anchor: TRANSCRIPT_MOV-01

Purpose

Introduces the foundational Montessori concept that the child must gain control of equilibrium through effort. Frames movement as essential to development, not something adults should interrupt or replace.

Materials

None explicitly listed.

Presentation

Gather the children in an open space and discuss the biological need for movement.
"Have you ever noticed how your body wants to move even when you are sitting still? In Montessori, we know that movement is how we build our minds and our coordination."
Explain the concept of Equilibrium through Effort.
"When we walk or run, we are constantly working to keep our balance. This effort is what helps us grow strong and precise in our music and our work."

Reflections for the Album

  • How does the child’s movement change from the first to the second plane?
  • Why is it important for the adult not to interrupt the child’s physical explorations?
  • Observe a child "walking on the line" vs. moving in the Elementary community—what differences do you see in their intention?
"Movement is a tool for the intellect. Without it, the mind remains isolated. In the Elementary years, this movement must be social and artistic, moving beyond the simple 'analysis of movement' seen in the Casa."
Elim Lee // April 1st Sprint // AMI Elementary
