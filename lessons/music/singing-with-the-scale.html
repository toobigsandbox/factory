Singing with the Scale

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To bridge the gap between sensorial tone bar experience and lyrical composition using a simple one-note-per-syllable framework.

Presentation

Set up the diatonic tone bars and demonstrate the scale.
"Let's hear the scale: 1-2-3-4-5-6-7-8 and back down. We can use these numbers to build a song about our day."
Model a composition based on a classroom event (e.g., a missing student).
"Let's write a song for Brandon who is away today. One syllable per note:
1-2-3-4-5-6-7-8: 'Bran-don is not with us to-day'
8-7-6-5-4-3-2-1: 'We will miss him while he’s a-way'"

Invite the children to write and play their own.
"You don't need note names, just numbers 1 through 8. Write the numbers above your words and we can play it together."
"Required Illustration: Write out one student-created song using the lyrics + numbers format. Support children in pairs—one playing while the other sings. Never force solo performance; keep it safe and communal."
