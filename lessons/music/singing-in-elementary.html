Music // Repertoire

Singing in the Elementary

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To broaden the musical repertoire and connect singing to the cultural, social, and emotional life of the community.

Presentation

Discuss the breadth of genres available.
"We sing classical songs, rounds, folk songs from our area, and even silly songs. Music represents the cultures in our classroom and the world."
Introduce a favorite "silly song" to break the tension of perfection.
"Elementary children often love humor. Let's sing 'Nobody Likes Me'—it's a beloved classic for this age group."
Nobody Likes Me, Everybody Hates Me

Nobody likes me, everybody hates me,
I guess I’ll go eat worms.
Long thin slimy ones, short fat juicy ones,
Itsy bitsy fuzzy wuzzy worms.
Down goes the first one, down goes the second one,
Oh how they wiggle and squirm!
(Repeat with playful variations)
Connect songs to current events and lived experiences.
"If we are excited about a nature walk, we sing 'Down by the Creek' before we go. This deepens the connection between life and song."
Diversity of genre creates a rich vocal landscape
"Sing every day. Let it be a rhythm in your classroom life. Even imperfect voices build perfect moments of connection. As children reach 9-12, expand into more complex rhythms and broader vocal ranges."
Elim Lee // Singing Module // AMI Elementary
