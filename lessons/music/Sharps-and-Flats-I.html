Music // The Tone Bars

Tone Bars (1): Matching and Grading

Guide: Elim Lee // AMI Elementary Submission

Purpose

To refine the child's auditory sense through the matching and grading of musical pitches, laying the sensorial foundation for melody and scales.

Materials

  • Set of Tone Bars: White, black, and grey blocks.
  • Mallet and Damper: For striking and silencing the bars.
  • Green Rug: To define the workspace.

Presentation

Invite the children to the rug. Set out two sets of Tone Bars in random order.
"We have listened to the sounds in our room. Now we are going to find sounds that are twins."
Strike one bar from the first set. Listen until the sound fades.
"That sound has a match somewhere in this other set. Let's find it."
Fig 1.1: Striking the control bar to find a match
Strike a bar from the second set. Compare. If they match, place them side-by-side.
"These two sounds are the same. They are twins."
Once matched, shuffle one set and invite the child to arrange them from the lowest sound to the highest.
"Now we will make a staircase of sound, from the deepest low to the highest high."
"Note to the Guide: Always handle the bars by the wooden blocks, never the metal. The oil from fingers can affect the pitch over time. Ensure the child uses the damper to stop the sound completely before striking the next bar."
Elim Lee // Semester 4 Sprint // AMI Elementary
