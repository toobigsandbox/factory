Balancing Equations: Addition

The Equation as a Logical Puzzle

Purpose

To introduce the logic of balancing equations through concrete manipulation of bead bars, demonstrating that an equation must have equal value on both sides.

Materials

Felt mat, number cards, arithmetic signs (+, =), basket of bead bars (1-9).

Presentation

Assemble a small group. Lay out a pink 3-bar and a light blue 5-bar with a plus sign, an equal sign, and a brown 8-bar.
"I am going to read you some math. Three plus five equals eight. We can say this is an equation because the value on this side is exactly the same as the value on that side."
Invite a child to "unbalance" the equation by adding a green 6-bar to the left side.
"Now we have nine plus six plus three equals nine... wait, that isn't right! Is our equation balanced? No. How can we fix it?"
Allow the children to suggest solutions. Guide them to add a 6-bar to the right side to restore balance.
"Now we know how to unbalance and then rebalance an equation using addition. One of you can write the equation, one can unbalance it, and the other can try to fix it!"
"Algebra is presented here as a game or a mystery to be solved. Empower children by using the formal term 'Algebra'—they feel a great sense of achievement tackling a subject usually reserved for older students."
Elim Lee // Algebra Module // AMI Elementary
