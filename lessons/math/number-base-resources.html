Number Base Resources

Material Overview & Teacher Readiness

Material Analysis

Bead Bars: Traditional Montessori colors (1-Red, 2-Green, 3-Pink, etc.) are used, but specifically grouped into non-decimal sets.

Base Conversion Cards: Wooden cards that allow the child to place place-value symbols (e.g., $3^2, 3^1, 3^0$) over the digits to visualize the conversion.

Fig 1.1: Standardized Layout for Bead Bar Photos

Teacher Prep Script

  • Identify the target base and prepare the corresponding bead bars (groups).
  • Ensure the "Decimal Bridge" is explained conceptually before providing the cards.
  • Set up four photo shots: Close-up of Base-3 bars, the full conversion algorithm on paper, the wooden cards in use, and the final checked result.
"Don't rush to the algorithm. Let the children physically group the beads into 'threes' or 'fives' until their hands understand the division before their pencils do."
