Geometry // Volume Narrative Step 3

The Three Dimensions of Volume

Building the "Skeleton" of a Solid

Material Analysis

2-centimeter unit cubes, a solid rectangular prism, pencil, tickets for labeling.

Presentation

Pick up the cubes and begin building along the edges of the solid prism.
"Suppose we didn't have our yellow measuring material. We could still find the volume by building a little skeleton with these cubes. Let's count how many cubes fit along each edge."
FIG 3.1: BUILDING THE SKELETON WITH CUBES
"We have five units here for length, five units for width, and ten units for height. When we multiply these three dimensions together—$5 \times 5 \times 10$—we get 250 unit cubes."
"Make sure the children stack the cubes visibly. They shouldn't build 'blindly' behind the prism; the whole point is for them to see the three directions the space expands."
