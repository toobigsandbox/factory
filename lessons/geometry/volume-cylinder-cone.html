Geometry // Volume Narrative Step 9

Volume of Cylinder & Cone

Expanding Prismatic Formulas to Curvilinear Solids

Material Analysis

Geometric solid cylinder, hollow cylinder, hollow cone (matching base/height), soil or sand, tray, ruler.

Part I: The Cylinder Formula

Hold up the cylinder. Point to the circular base.
- "To find the volume of any solid, we multiply the height by the area of the base. What shape is this base? Yes, a circle! And the area of a circle is $\pi r^2$."
Volume (Cylinder) = $\pi r^2 \times h$

Part II: The Cone (The 1:3 Relationship)

Pour sand from the cone into the cylinder to show that it takes three full cones to fill it.
- "Remember the pyramid? It was one-third of the prism. The cone has the same relationship with the cylinder! We can think of a circle as a polygon with an infinite number of sides."
FIG 9.1: POURING CONE VOLUME INTO CYLINDER
Volume (Cone) = $(\pi r^2 \times h) \div 3$
"Invite children to measure everyday cylinders—like water bottles or pencil cups—to calculate the volume. It makes the abstract formula tangible."
