Geometry // Introduction to Solids

Nomenclature of Solids

Identifying the Parts and Names of 3D Space

Material Analysis

Geometric solids (blue or wooden), mat, name labels (nomenclature cards), command cards for parts (base, face, vertex, edge).

Presentation I: The Whole

Invite the child to the mat. Choose three contrasting solids: Cube, Sphere, Cylinder.
"You’ve seen these shapes before in the primary classroom. Today we are going to learn their formal names and how we describe their parts."

Presentation II: The Parts

Using a rectangular prism, point to the flat surfaces, the lines where they meet, and the points.
"The flat surface is the 'Face'. The line where two faces meet is the 'Edge'. And the point where edges meet is the 'Vertex'."
"Don't provide all 10 solids at once. Start with a 3-period lesson on 3 solids, then expand. The child’s hand should 'travel' the edges and rest on the faces to sensorially distinguish them."
Elim Lee // Semester 4 Sprint // AMI Elementary
