Geometry // Further Explorations with Equivalence

Euclid's Plate

Guide: Elim Lee // April Submission Sprint

Direct Aim

To explore Euclidean geometry sensorially and begin to reason through geometric proofs.

Materials

Euclid Plate, metal squares, red rectangles, yellow parallelograms.

Presentation I: The Initial Setup

Set the plate on the table. Point to the different components.
"Euclid was a thinker who lived long ago. He wanted to show that the squares on the sides of a right triangle are equal to the square on the hypotenuse."
Fig 1.1: Hand pointing to the initial placement

Presentation II: Sensorial Proof (Sliding)

Slowly slide the parallelograms to show their equivalence to the squares.
"Watch as this square changes its shape, but keeps the same base and the same height. It transforms into a parallelogram."
Fig 2.1: The sliding motion and the "reasoning child"

The Conclusion

Sum up the findings. The red squares on the side equal the space of the hypotenuse.
"Because they have the same base and same height, they are equivalent. We have proven it just as Euclid did!"
Fig 3.1: Final arithmetic proof
