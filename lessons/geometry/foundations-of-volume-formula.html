Geometry // Volume Narrative Step 4

Foundations of Volume Formula

Transitioning from Concrete to Abstract Calculation

Material Analysis

Rulers or Montessori measuring tapes, variety of rectangular prisms, paper and colored pencils.

Presentation

Write "Base" on a piece of paper and label it with a small "a" for area.
"Remember how we found the area of the base? We multiplied the length by the width. Today, we are going to unify our formulas. When we multiply that base area by the height, we get the volume."
The General Formula
Volume = l × w × h
Show the alternative naming: Base Area × Height ($B \times h$).
"In some books, you might see it written as $B \times h$. The $B$ just stands for the length times the width. It's the same idea, just different words."
Demonstrate measuring a prism in centimeters and calculating the volume.
"I want you to go back to the prisms you built with cubes. Measure them with your ruler and see if this formula gives you the same answer we got by counting."
"Centimeters are the ideal unit here because they map perfectly to our 2cm unit materials. Encourage the children to write their final answer with 'cubic centimeters' or 'unit cubes' to reinforce the 3D concept."
